02 — The Foundation

The Power of the Prefix

Two ancient Greek words shape the entire lens through which we view modern economics. Changing a single prefix shifts the focus from the granular details of individual choices to the massive waves of national movements.

💡 Economics: The social science studying how individuals, businesses, and governments allocate scarce resources to satisfy unlimited wants
μικρός

Micro · small

Focuses on the behavior and decisions of individual units. It looks closely at how people and businesses allocate resources within specific markets.

μακρός

Macro · large

Focuses on the behavior of the whole system. It examines aggregate data to understand the overall performance and structure of a national economy.

03 · Branch One

Microeconomics

The Microscope: Individual Choice

Micro zooms in on the foundational building blocks. It studies how and why people and businesses make the choices that shape daily markets.

  • 01
    Consumer Behavior
    How individuals decide what to buy, save, or skip.
  • 02
    Supply & Demand
    Pricing forces for specific products in specific markets.
  • 03
    Firm-Level Decisions
    How a single business sets prices, hires, and produces.
Focus: One bakery. One product. One price.
Focus: Millions of decisions happening across a whole nation.
04 · Branch Two

Macroeconomics

The Satellite: National Stability

Macro rises high above the marketplace to evaluate the total "Aggregate". It gauges the health of an entire nation by tracking massive economic shifts.

GDP Total national output and wealth generation.
Inflation Pace of rising prices across the system.
Unemployment Percentage of labor force without work.
Global Trade International commerce and policy.

05 — Summary

The Complete Picture

Both branches study the same economy from different distances. Neither is more important than the other — you need both to truly understand how the world works.

🔍 Micro Questions

  • Why does this product cost exactly what it costs?
  • How do consumers decide what to buy?
  • How do businesses set prices for profit?
  • What happens when supply drops in a local market?
VS

🌐 Macro Questions

  • Why is the entire country experiencing inflation?
  • Why did millions lose their jobs in a recession?
  • Should the government raise or cut taxes?
  • How much is the nation producing in total (GDP)?
